Addition of two vectors and multiplication of a vector by a scalar. Algebra of vectors involving addition and scalar multiplication. The norm of a vector. The dotproduct and orthogonal vectors. Geometric interpretation of these concepts andtheir connection to the traditional method of representing a vector in terms ofstandard unit vectors. • The n-tuple as a generalisation of ordered triple and the space R n of all n-tuples. Addition of two n-tuples and multiplication ofan n-tuple by a scalar. Listing of the algebraic properties of R n thatmakes it a vector space. Dot product of n-tuples and orthogonality. The Cauchy-Schwarz inequality in R n . • Sub space of R n . Geometric meaning of subspaces in R 2 .and R 3 . Linear dependence and independence of vectors in R n . Basis anddimension and the standard basis of R n . Orthogonal and orthonormal bases. Representation of an arbitrary vector in an orthonormal basis. The Gram-Schmidtorthogonalisation process.
