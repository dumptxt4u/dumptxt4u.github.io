Module III - Vector Calculus (30 Hours) After the diﬀerentiation of vector valued functions in the last semester, here we introduce the concept of integrating vector valued functions. Some important theorems are also to be discussed here. The main topics are the following : Vector ﬁelds and their graphical representation, various type of vector ﬁelds (inverse- square, gradient, conservative), potential functions, divergence, curl, the 5 operator, Laplacian; Integrating a function along a curve (line integrals), integrating a vector ﬁeld along a curve, deﬁning work done as a line integral, line integrals along piecewise-smooth curves, integration of vector ﬁelds and independence of path, fundamental theorem of line inte- grals, line integrals along closed paths, test for conservative vector ﬁelds, Green’s theorem and applications; Deﬁning and evaluating surface integrals, their applications, orientation of surfaces, evaluating ﬂux integrals, The divergence theorem, Gauss’ Law, Stoke’s theorem, applica- tions of these theorems.
