Laurent Series

The Laurent series is a generalization of the Taylor series that allows for negative powers of (z - a).

Definition

If f(z) is analytic in an annulus R₁ < |z - a| < R₂, then it can be expanded as:

f(z) = Σn=-∞∞ cn (z - a)n

where

cn = (1 / 2πi) ∮ (f(z) / (z - a)n+1) dz

and C is a positively oriented contour within the annulus.

Structure

The Laurent series has two parts:

  • Regular (analytic) part: Σn=0∞ cn (z - a)n
  • Principal (singular) part: Σn=1∞ c-n (z - a)-n

Why it is useful

  • Describes functions with singularities (poles, essential singularities).
  • Essential in residue theory for evaluating complex integrals.
  • The c-1 coefficient is called the residue of f(z) at z = a.

Example

Expand f(z) = 1 / (z(z - 1)) around z = 0 for |z| < 1:

f(z) = 1 / (z(1 - z)) = (1 / z) * (1 / (1 - z))
      = (1 / z) * (1 + z + z² + z³ + …)
      = 1/z + 1 + z + z² + z³ + …

The 1/z term is the principal (singular) part, and the rest is the regular part.
