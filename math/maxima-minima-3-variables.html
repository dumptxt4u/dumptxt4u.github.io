1. Introduction

We study optimization of functions like \( f(x,y,z,w) \) in 4D space. The process generalizes single-variable calculus using gradients and Hessians.

2. Gradient & Stationary Points

The gradient is:

\[
\nabla f = \left( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial z}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial w} \right)
\]

Set all partial derivatives to zero:

\[
\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 0,\quad
\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 0,\quad
\frac{\partial f}{\partial z} = 0,\quad
\frac{\partial f}{\partial w} = 0
\]

3. Hessian Matrix

\[
H =
\begin{pmatrix}
f_{xx} & f_{xy} & f_{xz} & f_{xw} \\
f_{yx} & f_{yy} & f_{yz} & f_{yw} \\
f_{zx} & f_{zy} & f_{zz} & f_{zw} \\
f_{wx} & f_{wy} & f_{wz} & f_{ww}
\end{pmatrix}
\]

This determines curvature near critical points.

4. Classification

  • All eigenvalues positive → Minimum
  • All eigenvalues negative → Maximum
  • Mixed signs → Saddle point
Case Result
Positive definite Minimum
Negative definite Maximum
Indefinite Saddle

5. Lagrange Multipliers

Solve:

\[
\nabla f = \lambda \nabla g
\]

with constraint \( g(x,y,z,w)=c \).

6. Example

\( f = x^2 + y^2 + z^2 + w^2 \)

Gradient gives:

\[
2x=0,\;2y=0,\;2z=0,\;2w=0
\]

So minimum at \( (0,0,0,0) \).
