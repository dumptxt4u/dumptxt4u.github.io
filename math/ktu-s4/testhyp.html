🧪 Hypothesis Testing

Explained in Very Simple Words

What is Hypothesis Testing?

Hypothesis Testing is like being a detective with data.
You have a guess about something, and you use data to decide whether your guess is probably true or probably false.

Real-life Example:

A company says: “Our new energy drink makes people run faster.”
You don’t just believe them. You test it!

You give the drink to some runners and measure their speed. Then you check if the improvement is real or just by chance.

The Two Main Guesses (Hypotheses)

  • Null Hypothesis (H₀) → "Nothing is happening" or "No difference"
    Example: “The energy drink does NOT make people faster.”
  • Alternative Hypothesis (H₁) → "Something is happening"
    Example: “The energy drink DOES make people faster.”

Simple Steps of Hypothesis Testing

  1. Make your guess (Null and Alternative Hypothesis)
  2. Collect data from real world
  3. Calculate a special number (called test statistic or p-value)
  4. Decide: Is the result surprising enough to reject the Null Hypothesis?

Decision Time

If the data strongly disagrees with your Null Hypothesis (H₀), you Reject H₀.
That means you believe the Alternative Hypothesis (H₁) is probably true.

If the data is not strong enough, you Fail to Reject H₀.
You don’t say it’s true — you just say there’s not enough proof to say it’s false.

Like in Court:

Null Hypothesis = “The person is innocent”
You need strong evidence to reject “innocent” and say “guilty”.
If evidence is weak → You don’t reject innocent.

Very Important Words

  • p-value: How likely the result happened just by chance. Small p-value = surprising result.
  • Significance level (α): Usually 5% (0.05). If p-value < 0.05, we reject H₀.

In short: Hypothesis Testing helps us make smart decisions using data instead of just guessing!
