5 More Important Questions on Testing of Hypothesis

(Questions 6-10 – Frequently asked in KTU S4 MAT202 Exams)

6. Explain the difference between One-tailed and Two-tailed tests with examples.

Answer:
Two-tailed test: Used when H₁ has ≠ sign. Rejection region is on both tails of the distribution.
Example: H₀: μ = 50, H₁: μ ≠ 50

One-tailed test: Used when H₁ has > or < sign. Rejection region is on only one side.
• Right-tailed: H₁: μ > 50
• Left-tailed: H₁: μ < 50

Note: For the same α, critical value changes (e.g., for α=0.05, two-tailed ±1.96, one-tailed +1.645 or -1.645).

7. A random sample of 400 items has mean 55 and standard deviation 20. Can we conclude that the population mean is 60 at 5% level of significance? (Large sample Z-test for mean)

Answer:
H₀: μ = 60  H₁: μ ≠ 60 (Two-tailed)
α = 0.05, Critical values = ±1.96

Z = (x̄ - μ) / (σ / √n) = (55 - 60) / (20 / √400) = (–5) / 1 = –5

|Z| = 5 > 1.96 → Reject H₀

Conclusion: The population mean is significantly different from 60 at 5% level of significance.

8. In a random sample of 200 bulbs, 160 were found to be defective. Test whether the proportion of defective bulbs is 0.75 at 1% level of significance. (Z-test for proportion)

Answer:
H₀: p = 0.75  H₁: p ≠ 0.75 (Two-tailed)
α = 0.01, Critical values = ±2.58

Sample proportion ˆp = 160/200 = 0.80
Z = (ˆp - p) / √[p(1-p)/n] = (0.80 - 0.75) / √[0.75×0.25/200] = 0.05 / 0.0306 ≈ 1.633

|1.633| < 2.58 → Do not reject H₀

Conclusion: The proportion of defective bulbs does not differ significantly from 0.75.

9. A sample of 10 items has mean 18.4 and standard deviation 3.2. Test the hypothesis that the population mean is 20 at 5% significance level. (Small sample t-test)

Answer:
H₀: μ = 20  H₁: μ ≠ 20 (Two-tailed)
α = 0.05, df = 9, Critical t-value = ±2.262

t = (x̄ - μ) / (s / √n) = (18.4 - 20) / (3.2 / √10) = (-1.6) / 1.012 ≈ -1.581

|–1.581| < 2.262 → Do not reject H₀

Conclusion: There is not enough evidence to say that the population mean differs from 20.

10. Write short notes on:
a) Critical region
b) p-value
c) Power of a test

Answer:
a) Critical Region (Rejection Region): The set of values of the test statistic for which we reject the null hypothesis.

b) p-value: The probability of obtaining a test statistic at least as extreme as the observed value, assuming H₀ is true. If p-value ≤ α, reject H₀.

c) Power of a test: The probability of correctly rejecting H₀ when it is false. Power = 1 – β (where β is Type II error probability). Higher power is desirable.

KTU Exam Tip: Always clearly write H₀, H₁, α, test statistic formula, calculated value, critical value/table value, decision, and conclusion in words.
