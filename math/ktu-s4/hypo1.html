5 Important Questions on Testing of Hypothesis

(Frequently asked in KTU S4 MAT202 Exams)

1. Define Hypothesis Testing. What is Null Hypothesis and Alternative Hypothesis?

Answer:
Hypothesis testing is a statistical procedure used to make decisions about a population parameter based on sample data.

Null Hypothesis (H₀): The statement of "no difference" or "no effect". It is the hypothesis that is tested and assumed to be true.
Alternative Hypothesis (H₁ or Hₐ): The statement that contradicts the null hypothesis. It represents what we are trying to prove.

2. Explain the Level of Significance (α). What are the commonly used values of α?

Answer:
The Level of Significance (α) is the probability of committing a Type I error (rejecting H₀ when it is actually true).

Commonly used values:
• α = 0.05 (5%) — Most common in engineering and social sciences
• α = 0.01 (1%) — Used in critical fields like medicine
• α = 0.10 (10%) — Used in preliminary studies

3. List and explain the steps involved in Testing of Hypothesis.

Answer:
The standard steps are:
1. State the Null Hypothesis (H₀) and Alternative Hypothesis (H₁).
2. Choose the Level of Significance (α).
3. Select the appropriate test (Z-test, t-test, etc.).
4. Calculate the test statistic.
5. Determine the critical value or p-value.
6. Make decision: Reject H₀ if test statistic lies in rejection region (or p-value ≤ α).
7. Draw conclusion in the context of the problem.

4. Explain Type I error and Type II error with their probabilities.

Answer:
Decision H₀ is True H₀ is False
Reject H₀ Type I Error (Probability = α) Correct Decision (Power = 1 - β)
Fail to Reject H₀ Correct Decision Type II Error (Probability = β)
Note: α is called producer’s risk and β is called consumer’s risk.

5. A manufacturer claims that the mean lifetime of bulbs is 1500 hours. A random sample of 100 bulbs has a mean lifetime of 1480 hours with a standard deviation of 120 hours. Test the manufacturer’s claim at 5% level of significance. (Use Z-test)

Answer:
H₀: μ = 1500 hours  H₁: μ ≠ 1500 hours (Two-tailed test)
α = 0.05  Critical values = ±1.96

Test statistic Z = (x̄ - μ) / (σ / √n) = (1480 - 1500) / (120 / √100) = -1.667

|Z| = 1.667 < 1.96 → Do not reject H₀

Conclusion: There is not sufficient evidence to reject the manufacturer’s claim at 5% level of significance.

Tip for KTU Exams: Always write H₀ and H₁ clearly, mention α, show calculation of test statistic, compare with critical value, and give conclusion in words.
