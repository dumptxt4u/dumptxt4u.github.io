"Taxonomy"


"Taxonomy" is  classifying and naming living organisms, a system of organizing them into groups based on shared characteristics. It extends to a broader definition of classifying any objects or concepts, using a hierarchical structure to organize information and make it easier to understand and communicate, as noted by Wikipedia. For example, in biology, the Linnaean system of classifying organisms is used, moving from broad categories like domain down to the most specific level
